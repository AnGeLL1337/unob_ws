Posudek vedoucího

Oponent(i)
prof. Ing. Stodola Jiří, DrSc.

he final thesis corresponds to the written assignment and the student has fulfilled the set objectives. I consider the chosen solution methods to be very suitable, the partial conclusions in the individual chapters of the thesis are sufficiently justified and I consider them correct. The graduate student designed the original sampling methodology, routinely mastered the new measurement technology based on X-ray spectrometry and evaluated the obtained results for vehicles with gasoline and diesel engines. The reasoned conclusions that the X-ray spectrometer is suitable for laboratory analyzes and for using samples from six types of passenger vehicles and four types of military vehicles, including four LEOPARD 2A4 tanks, are valuable. He described the results and progressions of the increase of selected wear metals in detail and graphically displayed the increase over time. The conclusions of the thesis are justified and correct. The formal and graphic level of the work is at a very good level. The asset is a thorough elaboration of the solved problem, mastery of a completely new measurement technology, analysis and evaluation of a relatively large number of motor oil samples.Defense question: In the DP, the measured values ​​of wear metals are listed in a time series, briefly state what the limit (dangerous) values ​​are, e.g. Fe, Al, Cu, Si for diesel and gasoline engines? Conclusion: I recommend the final thesis for defense and grade it as: A
